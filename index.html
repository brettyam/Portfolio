About

Dawg Pizza Website

This was my first major project in web programming. It was made for a fictional pizza company and serves as more of a testament to the web technology skills I have learned. User experience was not the main focus for this piece but despite that, the site has seen many iterations and tweaks to make the overall navigation more comfortable. Available on the "Orders" page is a fully functional shopping cart using Javascript and AJAX. Feel free to play around with it!

Tess Rental

For this project, I worked with team and took the role as the main developer. We built this site for a real client that wanted a place where her potential customers could get view the apartments that she was leasing. I worked very closely with the designers on my team to keep the UI clean and understandable. However, my main responsibility was to develop the templates for each page via HTML, CSS, and Javascript, as well as implement the Google Maps API.

Encaption Design Specification

In my "Design Thinking" class, we were required to work with a disabled client and design a speech-to-text application prototype. This required us really build user empathy through user research, interviews with our client, and iterating over several prototypes. This particular document is meant to simulated a design specification that would ideally be passed on to a team of engineers for development. As such, it is meant to be meticulous and thorough.

Encaption Design Process Book

In this document, I walk you through my thought process over the ten week arc of designing Encaption. From high level UX decisions to the minute color of a simple UI button, I do my best to rationalize all of these choices in favor of the user, as well as give a little bit of my own commentary along the way. Also included are some user interview notes, personas I made, and several accounts of user testing.
